Fonts§

Font Sizes§

Please use only the font sizes listed below. Sticking with a pre-determined set of font sizes will ensure a consistent look-and-feel, while at the same time providing a sense of even vertical rhythm.

Font Families§

Body Copy

Body copy use the Open Sans typeface, designed by Steve Matteson. There are 10 fonts available: 5 weights, with 2 styles each.

Open Sans is a humanist sans-serif typeface used for the body text on the site. It has a vertical upright stress, large x-height, and wide apertures. Open Sans has a neutral, yet friendly appearance. Though sans-serif, it borrows some elements from serif typefaces. For example, it has a true italic and options for the use of a double-storey a and g.

Headings

Headings use the Bitter typeface, designed at Huerta Tipográfica. Only four fonts are available: normal and bold weights, with normal and italic style. The italic style is a true italic.

Futura (not used)

Futura is a geometric sans-serif typeface not used on the site.

Comprised of simple geometric shapes, near-perfect circles, triangles, and squares, Futura has a subtle thick/thin transition of weight, virtually no stress, and has an appearance of efficiency and forwardness. It has quite a low x-height and tall ascenders, which enhance the readability of large headings on the web.

The design of Futura avoids the decorative, eliminating nonessential elements, but makes subtle departures from pure geometric designs that allow the letterforms to seem balanced.

Colors§

Derivation of Color Palettes§

Each site uses four main colors: Primary, Secondary, Dark Gray, and Light Gray. The Primary and Secondary are given, and the Dark Gray and Light Gray are derived from those; see the formula below. Each of the four main colors is accompanied by two shades (darker) and two tints (lighter). The four main colors should be used for most design components, while the shades and tints may be used for smaller or less important components. Specifically, where necessary, Dark Gray s2 may be used for black, and Light Gray t2 should be used for white.

This formula generates the gray colors from the given main colors.

  1. Start with two colors: a Primary and a Secondary.

    • Primary
      hsl(210, 1, 0.4)

    • Secondary
      hsl(344.9, 1, 0.75)

  2. Mix in 25% of the other color.

    • hsl(219.1, 0.51, 0.5)

    • hsl(317.4, 0.35, 0.61)

  3. Desaturate each by 87.5%.

    • hsl(218.8, 0.07, 0.5)

    • hsl(320, 0.05, 0.61)

  4. Darken to 0.25 (Dark Gray) or lighten to 0.75 (Light Gray).

    • hsl(220, 0.07, 0.25)

    • hsl(320, 0.05, 0.75)

Shades and tints are generated by lightness.
